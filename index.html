02 — Five Moods
Every feeling
gets a shape.
Most apps treat every day like a full-energy day. Haru doesn't. You get five emotional starting points — and each one unlocks a different kind of day. No labels, no judgment. Just your pace.
The language matters: Haru never reflects your emotional state back at you. "Today: One Thing" — not "You're anxious." The app holds the shape, not the feeling.
I'm good, let's goFull Energy
Your full task list, deep work blocks, and ambitious goals. Everything's on the table today.
Tired but tryingGentle Pace
A lighter load. The essentials only. Breathing room built in. Showing up is enough.
Anxious / overwhelmedOne Thing
One task. That's your whole day. Everything else waits. You don't have to see the whole staircase.
Low and heavyJust Exist
No tasks. Just gentle reminders that you're here. One small win if you want it. That's all.
Scattered, can't focusShaped
Time-boxed micro-tasks. Short sprints. Structure to anchor a drifting mind.
03 — How It Works
One question.
Every morning.
Haru is a single daily ritual. Open the app when you wake up. Answer honestly. Everything else follows — automatically shaped around your answer. No setup. No system. Just one question.
01
Morning check-in
A notification at your preferred time. Open Haru. You see one question: how are you feeling today? Five options. Tap one.
02
AI reads your day
Haru scans your calendar, reminders, and inbox. It filters and re-orders your day based on your emotional state — not your productivity score.
03
Your day takes shape
A card appears. Your day shape at the top. One featured task. A short task list. A message written for how you feel. Nothing more.
04
Completion tracking
Check things off as you go. At the end of the day, Haru logs your shape and your completions — building a quiet picture of your patterns over time.
04 — Design
Physical.
Tactile.
Quiet.
Haru looks like a hotel check-in card because a hotel card is about today — not forever. It's given to you, you use it, and it's gone tomorrow. The receipt aesthetic makes the day feel real, bounded, and completable.
Built in Expo / React Native. Designed for iOS first. WidgetKit support planned. The visual language: DM Serif Display, Shippori Mincho, IBM Plex Mono. Paper grain overlay. No dark mode — cream only.
